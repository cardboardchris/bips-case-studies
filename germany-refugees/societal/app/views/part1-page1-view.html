You are the special adviser to the Mayor of the city of Erfurt, Germany, the capital of the Thuringia region, a city of 208,000 facing the prospect of receiving and integrating as many as 4,000 migrants and asylum-seekers. Your community is predominantly white and Christian, with a small number of immigrant families; as few as 500 Muslims live in the city, a number expected to increase eightfold by the end of the year. How do you integrate these predominantly Muslim, non-Western migrants into your society; how do you provide for their social welfare needs; and how do you maintain the existing fabric of your community, while doing “the right thing”? What is “the right thing”? How do you serve the people of Erfurt, and the needs of the migrants? How do you plan for a profound cultural shift that will play out on the streets of your city, much less the region of Thuringia, and Germany as a whole?

Erfurt faces significant challenges in the months ahead. You’ll need to ensure you’re providing for the needs of 4,000 new migrants, the existing population of Erfurt, and the region as a whole. Can Erfurt handle the influx—socially, economically, politically? How will you balance the needs of the new arrivals with the existing needs of Erfurt’s citizens? Will there be enough jobs, enough homes, enough services for everyone? Can Erfurt adapt to the new realities? You’ll need to canvas local politicians, business leaders, and citizen groups, identify their concerns and issues, and take these into account in formulating your strategy.
